The four pillars — EU AI Act obligations (all share Literacy, Article 4)
1
Prompt Engineering — Literacy. How to communicate with AI systems to get consistent, usable, and safe outputs. Understanding where AI is reliable and where human judgement is required. All four pillars build on this foundation. This is Article 4 of the EU AI Act — already in force since February 2025.
2
Data Analysis — Human Oversight. How to interpret, question, and supervise what an AI system does with data. Participants learn to read AI outputs critically, identify errors, and build workflows where a human reviews and approves before anything is acted on.
3
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) — Traceability. How to force an AI agent to ground every output in a source document — retrieving before generating. Every conclusion cites the document, section, and page it drew from. The full retrieval chain is logged and auditable.
4
Agents — Transparency. How to build autonomous multi-step AI systems that are explainable at every step. Participants build an agent that logs each action, exposes its reasoning, and produces outputs a regulator, auditor, or decision-maker can read and verify. High-risk obligations due December 2027*.
Sector working examples
AML transaction monitoring alert

InputA series of cash deposits, each below EUR 15,000, that individually do not trigger identification obligations but collectively suggest a structuring pattern, flagged for review.
AgentRetrieves the applicable AML regulation, analyses the structuring pattern, drafts an internal assessment memo with every claim cited to its source document.
CheckpointCompliance officer reviews the memo and all citations before any internal escalation or STR filing decision to the CRF (Cellule de Renseignement Financier).
OutputDocumented assessment, full audit trail, ready for CSSF inspection. Designed for compliance officers, AML analysts, relationship managers, and risk teams.
Contract or legal document review

InputA contract or legal document requiring review for key clauses, obligations, or risk flags.
AgentRetrieves, reads, and flags relevant clauses; identifies obligations and risk provisions; drafts a structured review memo with every finding cited to the source passage.
CheckpointThe lawyer reviews the memo and all citations. The agent output is a working draft; the lawyer's review constitutes the professional act before anything is relied upon or sent to the client.
OutputCited review memo, full chain of evidence. Designed for lawyers, paralegals, and legal ops teams.
CSSF sub-fund notification survey — pre-filling from prospectus

InputA fund prospectus (PDF) for a new sub-fund requiring CSSF approval, and the CSSF sub-fund survey Excel form with required fields covering general information, investment policy, portfolio managers, and targeted investors.
AgentReads the prospectus, extracts the data required for each survey field (fund name, governing law, SFDR classification, investment policy type, base currency, NAV frequency, portfolio manager details, etc.), and pre-fills the form with every field cited to the exact prospectus section it was drawn from.
CheckpointFund administrator or legal counsel reviews each pre-filled field and its citation before submitting the form to the CSSF.
OutputA pre-filled CSSF survey with full traceability from every field back to the prospectus. Designed for fund administrators, legal teams, and compliance officers preparing sub-fund notifications.
Due diligence document review

InputA document set for a transaction, audit, or advisory engagement requiring structured analysis across multiple sources.
AgentReads and cross-references the document set, identifies key findings, flags inconsistencies or risk factors, and produces a structured memo with every conclusion cited to the source document and page.
CheckpointSenior consultant or partner reviews the memo and all citations before any finding is included in a client deliverable.
OutputCited, auditable due diligence memo. Designed for audit, advisory, and transaction teams in consulting and professional services firms.
