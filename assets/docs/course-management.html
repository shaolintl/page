Your teams are already building AI pipelines — multi-step workflows where each step is a prompt or tool call built on the previous result. These are powerful, but most produce outputs nobody can verify. This session gives you the knowledge to evaluate what your teams and subcontractors produce, set the standards those pipelines must meet, and hold them accountable. No hands-on building. For executives who need to govern, not operate.
Who this is for
Chief Executive Officers · Chief Compliance Officers · Heads of Legal · Chief Risk Officers · Managing Partners · Any senior leader responsible for AI adoption across their organisation or who signs off on AI tools procured from third parties.
What the session covers
1
What compliant AI output looks like. You will know what to ask for and what to reject. Traceable outputs, cited sources, logged steps, human checkpoints. What a regulator expects versus what most tools actually produce.
2
How to evaluate tools your teams or subcontractors bring you. A practical framework for assessing any AI tool: what questions to ask, what documentation to require, and what red flags to look for — whether the tool was built in-house or procured externally.
3
EU AI Act obligations at the organisational level. What Article 4 requires of you as a deployer. What the December 2027 high-risk obligations mean for your organisation. What you must be able to demonstrate to your regulator, board, or auditors.
4
Setting standards for your teams. How to define what acceptable AI use looks like in your organisation: the documentation required, the approval process, and the audit trail that must exist before any AI output is acted on.
5
Q&A on your specific context. Open discussion on the AI tools and workflows already in use in your organisation and how to bring them into compliance.
What you leave with
  • The ability to evaluate any AI tool your teams or subcontractors produce or procure
  • A clear framework for what compliant AI output must contain: traceability, citation, human oversight, audit log
  • Knowledge of your organisation's EU AI Act obligations as a deployer, in plain language
  • The questions to ask and the standards to set — so your teams know exactly what is required of them
  • Confidence to answer any regulator, board member, or auditor who asks about your organisation's AI governance
  • A certificate of completion and session record suitable for regulatory documentation
